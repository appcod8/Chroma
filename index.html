Why You Can’t Find Chroma Browser on the App Store

Transparency note on App Store keyword visibility and competition.

Even though Chroma Browser is fully approved, it’s effectively invisible in App Store keyword searches.

Search for “browser” on the App Store and you won’t see it. Other apps in the same category appear — but ours doesn’t. Users can only find Chroma Browser if they know the exact name.

Why This Matters

  • Mandatory $99 Annual Fee: Developers must pay to keep apps available, even if Apple is withholding earnings.
  • Opaque Review Process: Updates and bug fixes can be delayed unpredictably, while Apple’s own apps face no such restrictions.
  • Keyword Suppression: Limits discoverability and stifles innovation.

We’ve filed formal complaints with both the FTC and the DOJ about these practices.

“Chroma Browser is fully approved, but buried in App Store search results, making it invisible to users searching for ‘browser.’ Apple’s control effectively erases new competitors, favoring entrenched apps and limiting consumer choice.”

We believe in transparency, fairness, and innovation. If you’re a developer experiencing similar issues, document your experience and consider reporting it — your voice matters.
